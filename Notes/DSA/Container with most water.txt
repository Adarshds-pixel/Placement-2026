--------------------------------
2. Container With Most Water (#11)
Difficulty: Medium
Pattern: Opposite Two Pointer
--------------------------------

Formula:
Area = min(height[left], height[right]) × (right - left)

Key Observation:
- Water level is always decided by the shorter wall.
- Width = distance between the two walls.

Algorithm:
1. Initialize:
      left = 0
      right = n - 1
      maxArea = 0

2. While left < right:

      area = min(height[left], height[right]) * (right - left)

      maxArea = max(maxArea, area)

      if height[left] < height[right]:
            left += 1
      else:
            right -= 1

3. Return maxArea.

Golden Rule:
Always move the shorter wall.

Reason:
- Moving the taller wall only decreases the width.
- The shorter wall limits the water height.
- Moving the shorter wall gives a chance to find a taller wall and increase the area.

Time Complexity:
O(n)

Space Complexity:
O(1)
